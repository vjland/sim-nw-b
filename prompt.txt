
TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION: NWB SIMU (NATURAL WIN BANKER SIMULATOR)

1. OVERVIEW
A high-fidelity Baccarat simulation dashboard renamed to NWB Simu. It is designed to analyze a specific betting strategy based on whether the previous hand was a "Natural" win (no third card engaged). The system simulates an 8-deck shoe with full casino-standard drawing rules.

2. UI & AESTHETICS
- Theme: "Zen-Dark" professional dashboard.
- Colors: Pitch Black (#000000) base, Ebony (#0a0a0a) panels, Burgundy (#8A244B) primary accents.
- Layout: Fixed-height viewport with tabbed navigation (Performance, Big Road, Game Log).
- Icons: Lucide (trending-up, layout-grid, list).

3. CORE ENGINE
- Shoe: 8 decks, Fisher-Yates shuffle.
- Mechanics: 
  * Burn card sequence at shoe start.
  * Standard 3rd card drawing rules for Player and Banker.
  * Cut card padding (~14 cards) to end the shoe.
- Data Tracking: Detailed hand history including winner, scores, total card count, and unit profit/loss.

4. BETTING STRATEGY (NEW)
The simulation tracks the total number of cards dealt in the previous hand:
- If previous hand was a NATURAL WIN (Total Cards = 4, no 3rd cards drawn): Bet BANKER on upcoming hand.
- If previous hand was NOT a Natural (Total Cards = 5 or 6, 3rd card engaged): Bet PLAYER on upcoming hand.
- Ties: Ignored for strategy trigger (uses the last physical hand card count); existing bets on a tie result in a Push (0 PnL).

5. ANALYTICS & VISUALIZATION
- Performance Chart (Chart.js):
  * Fixed Y-Axis: -20 to +20 units.
  * Fixed X-Axis: 0 to 80 hands.
  * Real-time plotting of the cumulative profit/loss curve using the #8A244B accent.
- Big Road: A traditional Baccarat pattern grid (6 rows) visualizing streaks and chops with color-coded beads and tie badges.
- Game Log: A granular table showing every hand's result, the total card count (T-Cards), the bet placed, and the running balance.

6. SIMULATION CONTROLS
- Play/Pause: Real-time step-by-step simulation.
- Fast Forward: Instant calculation of the remaining shoe.
- Speed Control: Adjustable processing delays (Turbo, Fast, Normal, Slow).
